5 Tips To Support Your Mental Health

1

taking frequent short breaks

According to Oxford Learning, the best thing to do is to take short 5-10 minute breaks every 20-30 minutes. This can include going for a walk, stretching, talking to a friend, taking a quick shower or grabbing a bite to eat.

enjoy yourself and have a laugh :)

Did you know, that according to www.thegoodbody.com laughing is good for the heart and can increase blood flow by 20%? This is a reminder to have a laugh and crack a joke with friends, teachers, and your parents.

3

find a hobby or give to loved ones

Try to incorporate some daily physical activity into your routine, learn new skills, and give to others, since they have been proven effective to increase happiness.

being mindful and attentive

Mindfulness improves physical health. Mindfulness can: help relieve stress, treat heart disease, lower blood pressure, reduce chronic pain, improve sleep, and alleviate gastrointestinal difficulties.

5

eating healthy foods

Did you know that Bananas are an amazing healthy snack to eat and can boost your serotonin? This is because bananas contain tryptophan, a type of protein that the body converts into mood-lifting serotonin. Bananas also contain vitamin B6, which has been shown to even out your mood naturally and relieve symptoms of PMS.
