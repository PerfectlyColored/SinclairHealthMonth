Mental Health Week

From May 3-9, the Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA) and Sinclair Secondary School joins Canadians across the country in celebrating the 70th annual Mental Health Week. The focus of this year's Mental Health Week is to promote the importance of emotions and the role that understanding them plays in good mental health. Mental health is often stigmatized, and in this week we will be educating and destigmatizing the negative connotations associated with it! By destigmatizing mental health this month, people will be more willing to seek treatment and open to conversation.

Did you know that approx. 20% of Canadian youth are affected by a mental illness or disorder...?

Mental health is just as important as physical health.

It's important to be aware about mental disorders like:

  • Mood disorders (such as depression or BPD)
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Personality disorders
  • Eating disorders
  • Trauma-related disorders
  • Schizophrenia
  • Substance abuse
know the signs

signs of poor mental health / mental disorders

physical signs

  • sleeplessness
  • difficulty maintaining a healthy appetite or experiencing significant weight loss
  • panic attacks
  • restlessness or agitation

dangerous behaviours

  • using drugs or alcohol as coping mechanisms
  • anger and violent outbursts
  • engaging in risk-taking behaviours

social signs

  • fear of being around others, even children or family
  • mistrust of people they normally confided in or counted on
  • a sense of guilt and unworthiness

mental signs

  • unable to focus
  • lack of interest in activities that once brought enjoyment
  • struggling to work, parent or keep up at home
  • unable to handle stress with normal coping strategies
